Australia technically has 2 national animals. The emu and the red kangaroo. The red kangaroo. The red kangaroo can be anywhere from around one meter tall to one meter and seventy centimeters and the females weight anywhere between 18- 40kg and the males weight between 55 – 90kg wich is almost double the amount of the females.

The red kangaroos to speeds of almost 60km/h. They have a population of 11.5 million red kangaroos.



The National bird is the emu: The emu is a flightless bird it can grow up to six feet tall and weight more than 45kg.

If needed they can sprint up to 50 km/h. The emu is an important cultural icon of Australia, appearing on the coat of arms and various coinages. The bird features prominently in Indigenous Australian mythologies.

It represents resilience an renwal wich makes it a important cultural icon for indiginoues people an australians.

The Great Emu War took place in 1932 after world war 1 a lot of veterans were given land to farm, and by the time of the Great Depression they face challenges like falling wheat prices an failing crops.

Because of these challenges around 20000 emus immigrated to the region which caused a good amount of damage.

The military was called in to deal with the emu problem, and they used machine guns to try and curb the population.

Despite their efforts, the emus proved to be elusive and difficult to catch, leading to a series of failed attempts to reduce their numbers.

The Great Emu War ultimately ended in failure for the military, and the emus continued to thrive in the region.

The event has since become a humorous and somewhat legendary episode in Australian history, highlighting the challenges of wildlife management and the resilience of the emu population.
